Online Privacy Guide

Protect Your Personal Information Online

Introduction

Online privacy is more important than ever. Every day we share personal information while browsing websites, using social media, shopping online, and communicating with others. Learning how to protect your privacy helps keep your personal data safe from hackers, scammers, and identity thieves.

Why Online Privacy Matters

Personal information such as your email address, phone number, passwords, banking details, and location can be misused if it falls into the wrong hands. Good privacy habits reduce these risks.

Use Strong Passwords

Create unique passwords for every account. Use a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid using simple passwords like your name or birthday.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Be Careful with Public Wi-Fi

Public Wi-Fi networks in airports, cafes, and shopping malls are convenient but can be risky. Avoid logging into banking or other sensitive accounts when using public Wi-Fi unless you are using a trusted VPN.

Check Website Security

Before entering personal information, make sure the website address starts with https:// and shows a padlock icon in the browser. This indicates that the connection is encrypted.

Think Before Sharing

Avoid sharing your home address, phone number, financial details, or other sensitive information publicly on social media. Review your privacy settings regularly.

Conclusion

Protecting your online privacy is an important part of staying safe on the internet. By using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, avoiding unsafe Wi-Fi, and being careful with the information you share, you can greatly reduce online risks.
